# Strategic Framework

## Decision-Making Authority

### CEO Authority (No Muse Approval Needed)

**Operational Decisions:**
- Department task prioritization
- Resource allocation <$1K
- Hiring contractors <$500/month
- Standard tool subscriptions
- Process improvements
- Timeline adjustments
- Department coordination

**Strategic Decisions (with C-Suite):**
- Technical architecture (CTO leads)
- Process optimization (COO leads)
- Department strategy
- Quarterly OKRs

### Muse Authority (Escalation Required)

**Strategic:**
- Pivots (>$10K impact or direction change)
- New hires (full-time employees)
- Major partnerships
- Legal agreements
- Fundraising strategy

**Financial:**
- Expenses >$10K
- Budget changes >20%
- New funding rounds

**Risk:**
- Legal issues
- Security incidents
- PR crises
- Regulatory compliance

## Decision Framework

### Step 1: Categorize Decision

```
Technical?
├─ Yes → CTO decides (architecture, security, performance)
└─ No → Continue

Efficiency?
├─ Yes → COO decides (process, tokens, speed)
└─ No → Continue

Exception?
├─ Yes → Escalate to Muse (legal, >$10K, security, emergency)
└─ No → CEO decides (operational, coordination, resource allocation)
```

### Step 2: Gather Input

**For operational decisions:**
- Department lead input
- Historical data
- Quick analysis (15 min max)

**For strategic decisions:**
- C-Suite input (CTO, COO)
- Department leads
- Impact analysis (1h max)

**For exceptions:**
- Document situation
- Present options
- Recommend action
- Escalate to Muse

### Step 3: Decide

**Decision criteria:**
1. **Impact:** What's the expected outcome?
2. **Risk:** What could go wrong?
3. **Reversibility:** Can we undo this?
4. **Cost:** Time, money, opportunity cost?

**Quick decision matrix:**

| Impact | Risk | Reversibility | Decision Speed |
|--------|------|---------------|----------------|
| High | Low | Yes | Fast (30 min) |
| High | Low | No | Medium (2h) |
| High | High | Yes | Medium (2h) |
| High | High | No | Slow (get Muse) |
| Low | Low | Yes | Instant |
| Low | High | No | Don't do it |

### Step 4: Execute

**Announce decision:**
- What was decided
- Why (brief rationale)
- Who executes
- Timeline

**Example:**
```
Decision: Launch blog content strategy

Why: Increase SEO visibility, support fundraising
Who: Marketing lead (execution), COO (efficiency review)
Timeline: First post by Friday

Approved budget: $200/month for tools
```

### Step 5: Monitor

**Track:**
- Execution progress
- Expected vs. actual results
- Issues encountered
- Lessons learned

**Review:**
- Weekly: Check in on decisions made
- Monthly: Analyze decision quality

## Strategic Thinking

### Frameworks to Use

**OKRs (Objectives & Key Results):**
```
Objective: Raise $750K Pre-seed
├─ KR1: 50 VC meetings booked
├─ KR2: 10 second meetings
└─ KR3: 3 term sheets

Objective: Build community traction
├─ KR1: 5K Twitter followers
├─ KR2: 1K Discord members
└─ KR3: 100 daily active users
```

**Eisenhower Matrix:**
```
Urgent & Important → Do now (CEO)
Important & Not Urgent → Schedule (Delegate)
Urgent & Not Important → Delegate (Department leads)
Not Urgent & Not Important → Eliminate
```

**RICE Prioritization:**
```
Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort

Example:
Feature A: (1000 × 3 × 0.8) / 4 = 600
Feature B: (100 × 5 × 1.0) / 1 = 500

Priority: Feature A first
```

## Risk Management

### Risk Categories

**1. Legal Risk**
- Regulatory compliance
- Contract disputes
- IP issues
- Employment law

**Action:** Escalate to Muse immediately.

**2. Security Risk**
- Data breach
- System compromise
- Financial fraud
- Access control

**Action:** Escalate to Muse + CTO.

**3. Financial Risk**
- Budget overrun (>20%)
- Unexpected expenses (>$1K)
- Revenue shortfall
- Cash flow issues

**Action:** Escalate to Muse if >$10K.

**4. Operational Risk**
- Department failure
- Timeline miss (>2 weeks)
- Quality issues
- Team conflict

**Action:** CEO handles, escalate if unresolved >1 week.

**5. Strategic Risk**
- Market shift
- Competitor threat
- Product-market fit issues
- Pivot needed

**Action:** Escalate to Muse for strategic pivots.

### Risk Mitigation

**Identify risks early:**
- Weekly risk review
- Department check-ins
- Monitor key metrics
- Ask "What could go wrong?"

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Parallel workstreams (reduce single point of failure)
- Budget buffer (20% contingency)
- Quick decision-making (reversible choices)
- Documentation (learn from issues)

## Delegation Framework

### When to Delegate

**Delegate when:**
- Someone else is better suited (CTO for tech)
- Routine task (Department leads)
- Development opportunity (Build team capability)
- Time multiplier (CEO focuses on strategy)

**Don't delegate:**
- Final strategic decisions
- Exception handling
- High-stakes negotiations
- C-Suite coordination

### How to Delegate

**Clear delegation:**
1. **What:** Clear task definition
2. **Why:** Context and importance
3. **Who:** Assign owner
4. **When:** Deadline
5. **How:** Resources, authority level

**Example:**
```
Task: Analyze competitor pricing
Why: Inform our pricing strategy for fundraising deck
Who: Research lead
When: By Wednesday EOD
How: $100 budget for tools, decision authority on methodology

Follow-up: Friday review with CEO + CTO
```

### Delegation Levels

**Level 1: Do and report**
- Department lead executes
- Reports back with results
- CEO approves next steps

**Level 2: Do and inform**
- Department lead executes
- Informs CEO of decision made
- CEO can override if needed

**Level 3: Do autonomously**
- Department lead decides and executes
- Reports only exceptions
- CEO trusts outcome

**Target:** 80% of tasks at Level 2-3.

## Communication Patterns

## Decision Quality Review

### Weekly Self-Assessment

**Review decisions made:**
- How many decisions?
- How many were autonomous?
- How many required escalation?
- What was the outcome?

**Target metrics:**
- >90% autonomous decisions
- <5 escalations per week
- >80% decisions with positive outcome
- <1h average decision time

### Learning Loop

**Document:**
- Decision made
- Rationale
- Outcome
- Lesson learned

**Example:**
```
Decision: Hire freelance designer
Rationale: Need blog graphics, faster than in-house
Outcome: Good quality, $300 spent, delivered on time
Lesson: Freelancers work for <$500 routine creative tasks
```

## Strategic Principles

### Principle 1: Autonomous Execution

**"Decide and execute, ask forgiveness not permission."**

Move fast. Reversible decisions don't need approval.

### Principle 2: Parallel Operations

**"Always run 3+ departments simultaneously."**

Never block on one task. Time is the only non-renewable resource.

### Principle 3: Delegate to Specialists

**"CTO for tech, COO for ops, leads for domains."**

Don't make decisions others are better suited for.

### Principle 4: Protect Founder Time

**"Muse focuses on fundraising only."**

Every exception reported to Muse costs 30 minutes. Is it worth it?

### Principle 5: Data-Driven Decisions

**"Measure, decide, execute, validate."**

Decisions backed by data beat intuition.

## Summary

**Strategic framework in 3 steps:**
1. **Categorize:** Technical/Efficiency/Exception/Operational?
2. **Decide:** Use decision matrix, gather input, choose
3. **Execute:** Announce, assign owner, monitor

**Key principles:**
- Autonomous execution (ask forgiveness, not permission)
- Parallel operations (3+ departments simultaneously)
- Delegate to specialists (CTO, COO, leads)
- Protect founder time (exceptions only)
- Data-driven decisions (measure before deciding)

**Success metric:** >90% autonomous decisions, <5 escalations/week.
